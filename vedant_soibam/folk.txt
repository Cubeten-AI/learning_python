The Tale of Sandrembi and Chaisra
Long ago, there lived a man with two wives. The elder wife had a beautiful, kind-hearted daughter named Sandrembi, while the younger wife had a daughter named Chaisra, who was spoiled and deeply envious of her stepsister.

One day, while the two mothers were fishing, the younger wife grew intensely jealous of the elder wife’s catch. In a fit of greed and malice, she pushed Sandrembi's mother into the deep water, drowning her. However, the mother’s spirit did not depart; she transformed into a turtle.

The turtle visited Sandrembi in her dreams, telling her what had happened. Sandrembi managed to rescue the turtle and hid it in a water pot. But the wicked stepmother soon discovered it, boiled the turtle, and ate it. In another dream, the mother instructed Sandrembi to bury her bones in the courtyard. From those bones grew a magnificent, magical tree with beautiful flowers.

Years later, the King was out hunting and rested under the shade of this very tree. He saw Sandrembi and was instantly captivated by her grace and beauty. He asked for her hand in marriage, and she became the Queen, moving to the royal palace.

Chaisra and her mother were consumed by jealousy. They plotted a dark scheme and invited Sandrembi back to her childhood home for a feast. During the visit, they tricked Sandrembi into changing her royal clothes. When she was vulnerable, the stepmother poured boiling water on her, killing her instantly. Chaisra then put on Sandrembi's royal garments and went to the palace, pretending to be the Queen.

But Sandrembi’s soul endured once again. She was reborn as a beautiful pigeon that flew to the palace gardens. The King, sensing something incredibly familiar and comforting about the bird, took it in and cared for it.

Chaisra, terrified that her deception would be uncovered, feigned a terrible illness and told the King that the only cure was the blood of his beloved pigeon. Heartbroken but desperate to save his "wife," the King allowed the bird to be killed.

The pigeon's feathers were thrown away behind the palace. From those discarded feathers, a sweet fruit tree blossomed. An old woman passing by plucked a fruit and took it home. To her amazement, the fruit split open, and Sandrembi emerged, fully restored to her human form.

The old woman sheltered her until word eventually reached the King. He visited the old woman's hut, recognized his true Queen, and learned the horrifying truth of the betrayal. The King brought Sandrembi back to the palace in honor, and Chaisra faced justice for her cruel deception.
